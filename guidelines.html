Dietary Guidelines

Summary of Natalie Crawford's The Fertility Formula.

Eat lots of

Raw fruits and vegetables (high in fiber, fructose)

  • Aim for 2× servings of fruits/veggies per meal
  • Organic preferred

Proteins (target 50–70g/day, at least 20g per meal)

  • Should be mostly plant-based proteins (too much animal protein can be harmful — 1× serving/day of animal protein is ideal)
  • Mainly poultry for animal proteins (avoid all red or processed meats like sausage, bacon)
  • Try to buy free-range or pasture-raised poultry
  • Incorporate plant-based proteins into every meal
  • Eggs are a good source to supplement plant-based protein (also high in vitamins)
  • Soy is also a good option for plant-based protein

Unsaturated fats

  • From plant-based sources and fish (especially omega-3s)
  • Avocados, nuts are a good source
  • Olive oil and avocado oil are best oils for cooking (canola oil also ok)

Complex carbs

  • Examples: whole grains, legumes, brown rice, sweet potatoes, peas, steel-cut oats, corn tortillas. High in fiber.
  • Avoid refined and ultra-processed carbs (white rice, white bread, baked potatoes, corn/cornflakes, flour tortillas)

Limit

  • Sugar — especially added or refined. Real sugars like honey, maple syrup, dates in small amounts. Avoid sodas and sugary drinks.
  • Dairy — limit to 1× per day; choose full-fat, not skim/low-fat. Plant-based milks are good alternatives (as long as no added sugar).

Notes on this recipe collection

  • Family of 3 • 30 minutes or less
  • No fish, no avocado, no beans
  • Skip any avocado garnish or bean variation listed in the source recipes
  • Omega-3s aren't covered by these dinners (no fish). Boost via walnuts/chia/flax as a snack or salad topper, or an algae-based omega-3 supplement (ask your doctor).
